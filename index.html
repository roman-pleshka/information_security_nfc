// THIS IS HOW MOST ATTACKS BEGIN

You just automatically opened a link — without reading, verifying, or thinking.

If this tag belonged to an attacker, your device could have already downloaded malware, visited a phishing site, or leaked your data.

// What could have happened:

🎣

Phishing

A fake login page — you enter your password and hand it straight to the attacker.

💀

Drive-by Download

Opening a URL can automatically trigger a file download onto your device.

🔗

Redirect Chain

The tag leads to a "safe" site that then redirects you to a malicious one.

📲

Settings Manipulation

NFC can send commands to change Wi-Fi, Bluetooth settings, or install profiles.

✓ How to stay safe:

01

Check the URL before opening — does it match the expected source?

02

Disable NFC when not in use — reduce your attack surface.

03

Don't scan tags in public places unless you know who placed them.

04

Use an NFC Reader app to preview tag content before your phone acts on it.
